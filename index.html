The Ledger of the Damned

Records of survival, failure, and justified atrocity.

Fantasy – Epic Tragedy

In empires powered by soul magic, there are no gods, only myths forged to sanctify murder.

Elysia comes of age untouched but owned, trained as ornament and remembrance. Lucius, a fading mage, and Eris, his silent protégé, must kill the innocent to escape the fate carved for them—or complete it.

A curse has been prepared. Casting it will end everything that still remembers.

Sci-Fi – Political Fiction

Earth is gone, burned from the sky.

What remains of mankind lives underground, watched by its own sins. The stars above are silent. No one knows if silence means peace, or extinction.

Ashlin, a tunneler—“rock bitch,” in her own words—endures the Siege Directive’s doctrine of endless preparation. But how do you defy extinction, when survival was the first mistake?

Glory to Mankind.

War – Speculative Realism

A genocidal war burns in the forgotten borderlands of the Veligrad Federation. Artem, 19, conscripted and already broken, is sent to the siege of the separatist capital—where ideals go to die.

Across the mountains, the neutral Republic of Seleneraad mobilizes its reserves. Not to intervene. Just to keep the refugees out.
